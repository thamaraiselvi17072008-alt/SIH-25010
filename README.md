## Problem Title
SIH 25010: Smart Crop Advisory System for Small and Marginal Farmers
## Problem Description
A majority of small and marginal farmers in India rely on traditional knowledge, local shopkeepers, or guesswork for crop selection, pest control, and fertilizer use. They lack access to personalized, real-time advisory services that account for soil type, weather conditions, and crop history. This often leads to poor yield, excessive input costs, and environmental degradation due to overuse of chemicals. Language barriers, low digital literacy, and absence of localized tools further limit their access to modern agri-tech resources.

Impact / Why this problem needs to be solved

Helping small farmers make informed decisions can significantly increase productivity, reduce costs, and improve livelihoods. It also contributes to sustainable farming practices, food security, and environmental conservation. A smart advisory solution can empower farmers with scientific insights in their native language and reduce dependency on unreliable third-party advice.

Expected Outcomes

• A multilingual, AI-based mobile app or chatbot that provides real-time, location-specific crop advisory.
• Soil health recommendations and fertilizer guidance.
• Weather-based alerts and predictive insights.
• Pest/disease detection via image uploads.
• Market price tracking.
• Voice support for low-literate users.
• Feedback and usage data collection for continuous improvement.

Relevant Stakeholders / Beneficiaries

• Small and marginal farmers
• Agricultural extension officers
• Government agriculture departments
• NGOs and cooperatives
• Agri-tech startups

Supporting Data

• 86% of Indian farmers are small or marginal (NABARD Report, 2022).
• Studies show ICT-based advisories can increase crop yield by 20–30%.

## Proposed Solution
<h3></h3>
<ul><li>Detailed explanation of the proposed solution
We propose developing an AI-driven, multilingual Smart Crop Advisory System accessible via a mobile application and IVR/voice assistant for inclusivity. The system will integrate soil, weather, and market data to provide personalized, real-time advice for crop selection, input optimization, and disease management. Key innovations include:

Voice-first design for farmers with low digital literacy.
Pest/disease detection through smartphone image recognition.
Localized recommendations in regional languages.
Offline functionality for rural areas with low connectivity.
Feedback loop to refine recommendations using AI/ML.</li>
<li>How it addresses the problem
### How it Addresses the Problem

1. **Knowledge Gaps → Personalized Scientific Advice**
   Farmers often depend on guesswork or local shopkeepers. The system replaces this with **AI-driven, data-based recommendations** tailored to soil type, crop history, and weather, ensuring scientifically sound decisions.

2. **Overuse of Chemicals → Optimized Input Use**
   The app guides farmers on the right quantity of fertilizers and pesticides, reducing costs and preventing soil degradation while boosting yields.

3. **Lack of Localized Tools → Multilingual & Voice Support**
   With support for regional languages and voice interaction, even farmers with low literacy or digital skills can access reliable guidance in their **own language and dialect**.

4. **Unpredictable Weather → Real-time Alerts**
   Weather-integrated insights provide timely warnings about rainfall, drought, or temperature shifts, helping farmers **plan irrigation, sowing, and harvesting** better.

5. **Crop Loss from Pests/Diseases → Early Detection**
   Using image recognition, farmers can upload pictures of affected crops and receive **instant identification and treatment guidance**, minimizing losses.

6. **Market Dependence → Transparent Price Information**
   Integration with market data helps farmers track daily mandi prices, **choose the right time and place to sell**, and avoid exploitation by middlemen.

7. **Connectivity Barriers → Offline Functionality**
   Offline mode ensures farmers in remote areas can still access advisory content, removing the dependence on constant internet connectivity.

Together, these features bridge the **trust, knowledge, and accessibility gap**, making farming more profitable, sustainable, and farmer-friendly.
</li>
<li>
### Innovation and Uniqueness of the Solution

1. **Voice-First, Multilingual Design**
   Unlike most agri-apps that are text-heavy, this solution prioritizes **voice interaction** in regional languages, making it accessible to low-literate farmers who form the majority.

2. **Integrated Multi-Source Advisory**
   The platform uniquely combines **soil data, weather forecasts, market prices, and pest/disease detection** into a single interface, ensuring holistic, context-specific advice instead of fragmented information.

3. **Offline-First Functionality**
   Rural connectivity is a persistent barrier. The solution provides **offline access with periodic sync**, ensuring advisory content is available even without internet.

4. **Farmer-Centric Feedback Loop**
   A continuous learning system where farmer feedback and usage data refine the AI/ML models, making the advice **progressively smarter and locally adapted**.

5. **Affordable and Scalable Model**
   Designed to run on **basic smartphones**, the app ensures scalability across millions of small farmers without high costs or need for advanced devices.

6. **Localized Pest/Disease Diagnosis**
   Real-time image-based detection combined with **region-specific treatment recommendations** is a novel feature, minimizing generic advice and ensuring precision farming.

7. **Trust and Community Building**
   By collaborating with government extension officers, NGOs, and cooperatives, the system creates a **trusted ecosystem** where farmers feel supported and confident to adopt scientific practices.

Together, these innovations make the solution not just another agri-advisory app, but a **localized, inclusive, and intelligent digital companion** for small and marginal farmers.
</li></ul>

## Technical Approach
<h3></h3>
<ul><li>Technologies to be used:
Technical Approach

Technologies to be used:

Mobile app: Flutter/React Native (cross-platform support)
Backend: Python/Django/Node.js with REST APIs
AI/ML models: TensorFlow/PyTorch for image recognition & predictive analytics
Databases: PostgreSQL for structured data; Firebase for real-time sync
Weather & market integration: APIs (IMD, Agmarknet, OpenWeather, etc.)
Multilingual & voice support: Google Speech-to-Text, Indic NLP libraries
</li>

Data collection & integration (soil, weather, crop, pest, market).
Model development (recommendation engine + pest detection).
App development (multilingual UI + voice assistant).
Pilot testing with small farmer groups.
Feedback integration and large-scale deployment. <b></b></li></ul>
